About Zoos

Zoos play a crucial role in today's world, as they provide care for injured animals that wouldn't survive alone in the wild. They help prevent species extinction by enabling endangered animals to reproduce in a safe environment. Visiting zoos offers a unique educational experience, allowing people to learn about animals from faraway places like Africa.

Zoos contribute significantly to wildlife conservation by offering a haven for endangered species to repopulate. They educate the public through animal encounters, guided tours, and summer camps. These programs bring awareness and understanding about various species and their natural habitats.

By implementing innovative breeding programs, reintroduction initiatives, and habitat restoration, zoos create a safe and enriching environment for their animals. Zoos contribute $22.5 billion to the US economy, provide jobs, attract tourists who spend money locally, and support educational outreach.
